A no-jargon guide to playing the videos from your home server (Jellyfin) on your Apple TV — using the easiest app, or the VLC you already have. Read it when you're tired; it's meant to just work.

The 30-second mental model

Two pieces talk to each other over your home Wi-Fi:

🗄️ The server (Jellyfin)Your own private Netflix. It runs on your home cluster and holds all your videos. You don't touch it — it just sits there serving.
📱 The app (on Apple TV)The "player." It connects to the server, shows your library, and plays a video on the big screen.

That's it. The app needs to know one address to find the server — that's the only setup.

⭐ The one thing to know — your server address

https://jellyfin.lan

Backup, if that doesn't work: https://192.168.5.173. Your Apple TV must be on the same Wi-Fi as the server. That's the whole secret.

A The easy way: the Swiftfin app RECOMMENDED

Swiftfin is a free app made specifically for Jellyfin on Apple TV. It's the nicest experience — posters, "continue watching," the works. This is the path to try first.

  1. On the Apple TV, open the App Store and search Swiftfin. Install it (it's free).
  2. Open Swiftfin. It'll ask to add a server — choose Add Server / Connect Manually.
  3. Type your address exactly: https://jellyfin.lan  (or the backup https://192.168.5.173).
  4. If it warns about a certificate (because it's your own private server, not a public website) — choose Trust / Continue. That's expected and safe on your own network.
  5. Log in with your Jellyfin username and password.
  6. Open the Hacker News FM library → pick a video → press play. 🎉
💡 Infuse is another popular Apple TV app that also works with Jellyfin — a fine alternative if you ever want to try one. But Swiftfin is free and purpose-built, so start there.

Why an "app" and not just a website?

Apple TV doesn't really do web browsers. So instead of visiting jellyfin.lan in a browser (which is how you'd see it on a laptop), you use an app that knows how to talk to Jellyfin. Same server, same videos — just a TV-friendly remote-controlled front door.

B The "I already have VLC" way

You've got VLC on your Apple TV and like it — great. VLC isn't a Jellyfin app, but it can still find your server over the network using something called DLNA (think: a way for media gadgets on the same Wi-Fi to discover and share with each other). One small one-time switch in Jellyfin, then VLC can browse it.

One-time: turn on DLNA in Jellyfin (from a laptop)

  1. On a computer, go to https://jellyfin.lan and sign in.
  2. Go to Dashboard → Plugins (or Playback on older versions) and enable/install DLNA. Save.

(If you can't find DLNA, don't sweat it — just use Swiftfin from Way A. DLNA is the only reason VLC needs this step.)

On the Apple TV, in VLC

  1. Open VLC.
  2. Go to the Network tab → Local Network.
  3. Your Jellyfin server should appear in the list (that's DLNA working). Open it.
  4. Browse into your videos and press play.
💡 Honest note: VLC will play the files, but it won't show the nice posters, descriptions, or "resume where you left off." For your AI videos with their cover art and metadata, Swiftfin (Way A) looks much better. Keep VLC as a handy backup.

? If it won't connect (quick fixes)

Server not foundMake sure the Apple TV is on the same Wi-Fi as the cluster. This is the #1 cause.
"jellyfin.lan" doesn't workType the backup address instead: https://192.168.5.173. (The .lan name only works if your TV uses your home network's name service.)
Certificate / "not secure" warningExpected — it's your own private server, not a public site. Choose Trust / Continue.
Wrong passwordUse your Jellyfin account (the one you log into at jellyfin.lan), not your Apple ID.
New videos missingThey appear automatically, but you can force it: on a laptop at jellyfin.lan → Dashboard → Scan All Libraries.

📖 Tiny glossary

Media serverThe computer program that stores your videos and serves them on demand. Yours is Jellyfin.
Client / appThe thing you watch with — Swiftfin or VLC on the Apple TV.
LibraryA collection inside Jellyfin. Yours is "Hacker News FM."
IP addressA device's number on your network, like a house address. Your server's is 192.168.5.173.
LAN / Wi-FiYour home network. Everything here only works when devices share it.
DLNAAn older "discover media on the network" standard. It's how VLC finds Jellyfin.
